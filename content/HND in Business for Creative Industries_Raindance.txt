About
Since 1992, Raindance has been at the forefront of independent film culture, championing low to no budget filmmaking and demystifying how the creative industries really work. This HND in Business for the Creative Industries is built on that legacy.
Creative industries demand bold thinkers and business leaders who understand both commerce and culture. The HND in Business for Creative Industries equips you with the practical business skills, entrepreneurial mindset and industry insight to thrive in today’s dynamic creative economy.
Designed for entrepreneurs, producers, managers, and creatives who want to understand the commercial engine behind film, television, and digital media, the course explores finance, funding models, distribution, marketing, intellectual property, and audience development. You will learn how creative projects are financed and sustained, from tax relief and private investment to festivals, sales, and alternative revenue streams. Drawing directly on Raindance’s experience running an international film festival, delivering industry training, and supporting independent creators for over three decades, the course blends real world case studies with practical projects.
Through a blend of real-world projects, strategic case studies and collaborative workshops, you’ll explore key areas such as marketing and branding, leadership, innovation, finance, digital business and creative enterprise management.
By the end of the programme, you will have a strong understanding of how to build, manage, and grow creative ventures, with the confidence to operate in a fast moving global industry. On completion of your HND, you have the option to progress to a one year BA [Hons] top up degree.

Key Information
The Higher National Diploma (HND) is an internationally recognised UK qualification that provides specialist vocational learning at levels 4 and 5. HNDs are delivered by universities and colleges, and are considered the equivalent of the first two years of a three-year university degree course. Students can often progress onto the final year of the BA Top Up degree programme to obtain a full B.A. (Hons).________________________________________
Overview
Designed for creatives. Built for industry.
What is the HND in Business for the Creative Industries?
The HND in Business for the Creative Industries is a higher national diploma that focuses on the commercial and entrepreneurial aspects of creative sectors such as film, television, digital media and cultural industries. It combines practical business skills with real-world industry insight, preparing students to manage, launch and grow creative ventures.
Who is this course designed for?
This course is designed for aspiring entrepreneurs, producers, managers and creatives who want to understand how creative projects are financed, marketed and sustained. It is ideal for those seeking careers in film, television, digital media, arts management or creative business leadership.
What will I learn on the HND in Business for the Creative Industries?
Students learn key business areas including finance, funding models, marketing and branding, distribution, intellectual property, audience development, leadership, innovation and digital business. The course focuses on applying these skills within real creative industry context
How is the course taught?
The programme is delivered through a mix of real-world projects, case studies, collaborative workshops and applied learning. Teaching is informed by Raindance’s extensive experience in independent film, industry training and international festival delivery.________________________________________
Programme Structure
Level 4 Year One
The Contemporary Business Environment
This unit introduces students to the economic, political, social and technological forces shaping modern organisations. It is explored through both traditional film, television, and creative media contexts and creative sector case studies, allowing students to understand how film, media, fashion and digital businesses operate within wider global markets.
Marketing Processes and Planning
This unit explores how organisations identify audiences, build brands and deliver value. The unit is contextualised through film marketing, festival promotion, digital campaigns and audience development, while remaining applicable to a wide range of business sectors.
Management of Human Resources
This unit focuses on managing people effectively within organisations. This unit examines recruitment, motivation and performance management through creative teams, freelance networks and project based working environments.
Leadership and Management
This unit develops an understanding of leadership styles and management approaches. The unit is delivered using creative industry scenarios such as production leadership, agency management and entrepreneurial ventures, alongside broader organisational examples.
Accounting Principles
This unit introduces core financial concepts including budgeting, cash flow and financial control. Teaching draws on production budgets, project finance and creative business models, while providing essential accounting knowledge transferable to any business environment.
Managing a Successful Business Project
This practical project unit enables students to plan and deliver a substantial business
project. Projects may include film and media ventures, film and television production plans or business proposals, developing skills in research, planning, delivery and evaluation.
Business Law
This unit explores legal principles affecting organisations, with particular focus on
contracts, intellectual property, copyright and consumer law. The unit is highly relevant to film, television, and creative media industries while remaining essential for all business students.
Digital Business in Practice
This unit examines how digital technologies transform business operations and revenue models. This unit explores streaming platforms and digital distribution channels, content distribution, online branding and monetisation, with applications across film, television, and creative media industries and wider digital economies.
Level 5 Year Two
Research Project
This Pearson set unit enables students to undertake an in depth investigation into a
business topic. Projects may focus on creative industry challenges such as funding models, distribution strategies or audience behaviour, while developing advanced research and analytical skills.
Organisational Behaviour Management
This unit examines how individuals and groups behave within organisations. The unit is contextualised through creative teams, temporary organisations and collaborative working, while offering insights applicable across all business sectors.
Managing and Leading Change
This unit explores how organisations respond to internal and external change. Creative industry case studies such as technological disruption, platform shifts and new business models are used alongside traditional organisational examples.
Global Business Environment
This unit explores international business operations and global markets. The unit is
delivered through examples such as international co productions, global distribution and cross border creative collaboration.
Principles of Operations Management
This unit focuses on planning, resource management and operational efficiency. In a
creative context, this includes production scheduling, logistics and delivery, while
remaining relevant to a wide range of operational settings.
Launching a New Venture
This unit develops the skills needed to launch a new business or creative enterprise. The unit supports start ups such as production companies, agencies, fashion labels and streaming platforms and digital distribution channels, while reinforcing core
entrepreneurial principles.

Admission & Entry Requirements
Is this course suitable for international students?
Yes. The HND in Business for the Creative Industries is internationally recognised and focuses on global creative markets, making it suitable for students seeking careers in the international creative economy.
Do I need prior business experience to apply?
No prior business experience is required. The course is designed to support both creatives new to business and those seeking to formalise their commercial knowledge within creative industries.

Progressions & Careers
Graduates can pursue careers in creative business management, production, arts administration, marketing, distribution, consultancy or entrepreneurship across film, television and digital media industries. The qualification also supports freelance and start-up pathways.

On successful completion of the HND in Business for the Creative Industries, students may progress to a one-year BA (Hons) top-up degree, subject to entry requirements.
