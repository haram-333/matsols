About
Looking to take your creative skills to the next level in the worlds of games, animation, or visual effects? The Raindance Film School Top-Up BA (Hons) in Games, Animation and Visual Effects is an intensive one-year degree designed for students with previous study in one of our HND pathways or professional experience in Computer Graphics who want to develop advanced specialisms and build a professional portfolio.
________________________________________
Key Information
This top-up degree combines creative practice, technical innovation, and industry insight to prepare you for a successful career in the fast-growing global entertainment industry. You’ll learn how to design, produce, and deliver high-quality projects that reflect the standards of professional games, animation, and VFX studios.

Programme Structure
Professional practice is at the heart of this course. You will create industry-relevant digital artefacts—from playable game prototypes and animated shorts to professional VFX sequences—as the main focus of your learning and assessment.
Students choose a specialisation—Games Development, Animation, or Visual Effects—and refine their creative, technical, and research skills through a combination of project work, academic study, and professional development.
You’ll work on independent and collaborative projects, explore the business and production aspects of digital media, and benefit from guest lectures, workshops, and one-on-one mentoring from industry professionals.
SEMESTER 1
Module 1: Critical Research (15 credits)
Investigate a topic related to your chosen specialisation—such as animation theory, gameplay design, or compositing techniques—through academic and practical research. You’ll develop skills in critical analysis, research design, and reflective practice, culminating in a written or visual research output that informs your final project.
Module 2: Specialist Negotiated Practical Project (45 credits)
Design and deliver a self-directed creative project based on your interests and career ambitions. This could include a playable game prototype, a short animated sequence, or a VFX compositing reel. You’ll manage the full creative process—from concept development and pre-production through to final output—supported by workshops and tutorials.
Your final submission will include your completed project, a process journal, and a critical evaluation of your creative workflow.
SEMESTER 2
Module 3: Film & TV Business Practices (15 credits)
Gain essential knowledge of the games, animation, and VFX industries, including studio workflows, job roles, production management, and intellectual property. You’ll study real-world business models, funding structures, and career strategies. Assessment may take the form of a case study, industry report, or video presentation exploring your chosen career pathway.
Module 4: Final Project – Film Practices (45 credits)
This is your opportunity to create a portfolio-quality project within your chosen specialism. Options include:
Games: Developing a playable game prototype using Unreal Engine or Unity
Animation: Producing a short 2D or 3D animated film
Visual Effects: Creating a professional composited VFX sequence integrated with live action
You will lead the full production process—from concept and design to post-production and presentation—showcasing advanced creative and technical skills. The final submission includes your finished artefact, documentation of your pipeline and workflow, and a critical reflection suitable for professional presentation or festival submission.

General Info & FAQs
How is the course assessed?
Assessment is via practical creative projects, written reflective defences of your work and research assignments. The weighting of this is approximately 70% creative practical projects and 30% written/ presentational (video/ audio) work.
How do I apply?
