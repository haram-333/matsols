About
Join the Raindance 4-Week Intensive Filmmaking Certificate, a hands-on programme designed for aspiring filmmakers, storytellers, and creatives eager to break into the film industry. Based in the heart of London, this immersive course covers screenwriting, directing, cinematography, producing, and editing, giving you the essential skills to bring your vision to life.

________________________________________
Key Information
Learn from industry professionals, collaborate with like-minded creatives, and gain practical experience in real-world filmmaking. Whether you’re a beginner or looking to refine your craft, this intensive program will equip you with the tools, knowledge, and confidence to create compelling films.

________________________________________
Overview
You will work on a number of short films, and take on at least one key role as a screenwriter, director, producer or cinematographer. The course is hosted by the team behind the world famous Raindance Film Festival in the heart of London, and is delivered by some of the industry’s top tutors.
Once you’ve successfully completed the course with a certificate and a short film, you can progress onto our HND in Film, Screenwriting, Directing and Acting.

Programme Structure
What will I study on the course?
Film Theory & Visual
Subtext Intro to genre, structure, and mise-en-scène
How visuals communicate meaning and emotion
Use of framing, movement, and colour for subtext
Symbolism, mood, and metaphor in film
Analysing iconic scenes for visual storytelling techniques
Applying theory to students’ own film ideas
Developing Ideas &
Scriptwriting Techniques Brainstorming ideas from experience or prompts
What makes a strong short film idea (unique, emotional, contained)
Basic structure: setup, conflict, resolution
Visual storytelling and writing for the screen
Writing loglines, outlines, and short scripts
Developing Characters Creating compelling, flawed characters with goals
Internal vs external conflict
Character backstory and motivation
Showing character through actions and choices
Exercises in dialogue and character development
Casting & Directing Actors Casting: how to run auditions and select talent
Directing actors with clarity and confidence
Rehearsals, blocking, and emotional beats
Building trust and safe spaces on set
Filming test scenes and giving direction
Pre-Production Finalising script, shot list, and schedule
Visual planning: storyboards and look references
Location scouting, permissions, and equipment prep
Roles, crew planning, and health & safety
Organising production documents
Camera Techniques &
Settings Basic camera settings: ISO, aperture, shutter speed
Composition and framing for story and emotion
Movement: handheld, tripod, sliders
Manual focus, B-roll, and coverage strategies
Shooting simple scenes and reviewing footage
Cinematic Lighting Natural vs artificial lighting
3-point lighting and its variations
Using light for mood, tone, and style
Colour temperature, gels, shadows, and contrast
Low-budget lighting tips and practical setups
Recording Audio Microphone types and best use cases
Capturing clean dialogue and ambient sound
Boom mic operation and field recorders
Avoiding common sound issues
Syncing audio and preparing for post-production
5 Days of Filming Students shoot short films in groups
Applying all learned skills on set
Managing time, resources, and collaboration
Capturing coverage and maintaining continuity
Adapting to real-world challenges
5 Days of Editing Importing footage and creating timelines
Rough cuts, pacing, and refining edits
Basic sound design and music
Colour correction and titles
Exporting final cuts and peer feedback
Workshop with Elliot Grove & Short Film Screenings Elliot’s insights on indie filmmaking and producing
Industry advice and open Q&A
Screening all student films
Feedback, celebration, and reflection
Next steps: festivals, careers, and development

Progressions & Careers
Once you’ve successfully completed the course with a certificate and a short film, you can progress onto our HND in Film, Screenwriting, Directing and Acting.
Where next? Further studies
After completing the certificate, students will be sufficiently trained to start working on their own film projects.
Upon successful completion and approval, students who wish to do further studies can progress onto the Raindance HND Level 5 Diploma in Film. Please note, you must be proficient in English and demonstrate the ability to study at level 5 to be considered for our HND programmes. This will be assessed during the course.

General Info & FAQs
What days do I go in?
The course runs Monday to Friday from 10.30am – 5.30pm over 4 weeks (20 consecutive weekdays). The course runs in August 2026.
What is the typical class size?
We aim to restrict the class size to 16 per group. This allows us to run practical workshops and give all our students on-going hands-on support throughout their learning.
Course Tutors
Josh Farrell is a screenwriter, director, and Raindance lecturer completing a PhD in screenwriting. His feature debut Sock was made for £2,000, and he’s now developing Socks and Pretty Killer. His work blends genre storytelling with social themes.
Ufuk Gokkaya is a guerrilla filmmaker with 20 years of experience in directing, shooting, and editing music videos, shorts, and corporate content. He recently produced his first feature and is studying for a PhD in filmmaking. Ufuk lectures at Raindance and specialises in low-budget film productions.
Cerys Knighton is an award-winning actor, director, and producer, and a graduate of the NFTS. Her short I’m Not Brilliant has won at global festivals, and her TV pilots Foiled and Hush reached the finals of major writing competitions, including Shore Scripts and Female Pilot Club.
Joshua Armstrong is an award-winning filmmaker known for short films exploring mental health through magical realism. He’s won two Pinewood awards and Best Short at the Fortean Film Festival, and is in pre production on two feature films. His background spans directing, VFX, editing, and pre-production.
Asaturs Keim is a filmmaker and BA Programme Leader at Raindance, known for blending analogue aesthetics with digital media. Passionate about cinematography and authentic storytelling, he champions collaborative, independent filmmaking rooted in human connection and societal dialogue.
Stuart Laing is a veteran actor with over 30 years in film, TV, and theatre, including a regular role in EastEnders. His credits include Spooks, Casualty, Vera, and Disney+’s Suspect. He’s also a drama school teacher and has performed at the National Theatre and Chichester Festival.
